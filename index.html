Who We Are

A Registered Kenyan Institution.
Built to Last Decades.

Orphanaid Kenya is a Company Limited by Guarantee, incorporated in Kenya on 28 October 2021 (Registration: CLG-BXFAQ6). We are not a temporary relief effort. We are building a permanent, professionally governed, internationally compliant residential institution for orphaned and highly vulnerable girls in the Kiambu Highlands north of Nairobi.


Our campus will be entirely self-sufficient — powered by off-grid solar energy, fed by a borehole water system and two acres of agricultural land, and governed to the standards of the Kenya Children Act 2022 and the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child.

The Development Model

Four Phases.
One Permanent Legacy.

Orphanaid Kenya is designed as a disciplined, phased institution. Each phase delivers documented outcomes that unlock the next. This is how you build something that lasts.

01
Foundation
Year 1–2 · USD 500,000
  • 8-acre Kiambu/Limuru land acquisition
  • Off-grid solar power system
  • Borehole water & 50,000L storage
  • Pilot dormitories — 10–20 girls
  • Security, kitchen, administration
02
Pilot Centre
Year 2–3
  • Full safeguarding protocols
  • Education & healthcare launch
  • Trauma-informed rehabilitation
  • Documented outcome framework
  • Staff training — international standards
03
Education & Self-Sufficiency
Year 3–5
  • Computer lab, library, workshop
  • Livestock & market gardens
  • Indoor theatre & sports hall
  • Medical clinic expansion
  • Scale to 50–80 girls
04
Legacy & Expansion
Year 5+
  • Full capacity — 80–100 girls
  • Community outreach
  • Social enterprise components
  • Reduced donor dependency
  • First cohort graduates
